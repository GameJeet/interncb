Account lockout

5 consecutive failures on one account locks it for 2 minutes, regardless of session.

Why these attacks fail against good defenses

  • Dictionary attacks rely on weak, predictable passwords. A strong password policy removes most of the wordlist’s matches.
  • Brute-force attacks need many attempts. Rate limiting and account lockouts make this slow and noisy.
  • Automated scripts struggle with CAPTCHA challenges, which require human-style input.
  • Every attempt is logged, giving defenders visibility into ongoing attacks via the Attack Logs page.
